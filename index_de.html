Have you ever thought about what would happen if one of the participants suddenly had a stroke and fell over?
The emergency QR code could be a solution!



How does this work?

We use almost all virtual backgrounds in the video conferences. With our body we cover the middle part of the background.
In a medical emergency, if someone falls over in front of the screen, the entire background becomes visible. The idea is now to store emergency information in this area, such as an address.
With a QR code, the information is not immediately legible if you get up for a moment. For maximum data protection, however, you should switch off your camera when you leave the picture.


3 steps to the goal

Step1 1

What information should be hidden in the QR code? For example, the address where you are could be useful. For example, go to Google Maps and select the address you are working on. Click on the share icon and copy the link into the following field:

Step2 2

From your computer, select the background image on which you would like the QR code to be placed.
Pay attention to the aspect ratio of your picture. Ideally, it should be 1920x1080 pixels.
